Feral pigeons (Columba livia domestica) are birds derived from domestic pigeons that have returned to the wild. 
Originally bred from the wild rock dove, which naturally inhabits sea cliffs and mountains   , 
these pigeons use the ledges of buildings as a substitute for cliffs. They have become adapted to urban life, 
preying on insects and scavenging. They are abundant in towns and cities throughout much of the world.
